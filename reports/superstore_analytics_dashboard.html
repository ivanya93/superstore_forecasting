🔍 Finding

Orders with >20% discount represent 11.4% of all orders but generate negative profit. The break-even point is a 20% discount ceiling.

💡 Implication

Sub-categories already running negative margins (Tables −8.6%, Bookcases −3%) should have discounts immediately capped at 0%. Any discount on these is a direct cash loss.

🏷️ Action 1 — Discount Policy Reform

Observation: 1,137 orders (11.4%) carry >20% discount and generate average losses of $77–$107/order. Tables sub-category alone runs −8.6% margin before discounts.

Action: Cap discounts at 20% company-wide. Eliminate discounts entirely on Tables, Bookcases, Supplies.

Expected impact: Recover ~$30–40K/year in margin from Furniture sub-categories.

📡 Action 2 — Channel Budget Reallocation

Observation: Display advertising costs $40.15/customer and yields only LTV:CAC 30×. Email costs $5.97/customer with LTV:CAC 101×. Direct channel: $3.32 CAC, 174× return.

Action: Reallocate 30% of Display budget to Email and Organic. Grow Direct referral programme.

Expected impact: Same customer acquisition volume at ~35% lower total CAC spend.

🎯 Action 3 — Retention Programme

Observation: 27 At-Risk customers (avg −$206 monetary, lapsed 376 days) and 150 Needs Attention customers (avg $3 monetary, lapsed 248 days) represent untapped recovery value.

Action: Targeted re-engagement campaign for Needs Attention segment. If 20% convert to Loyal, expected uplift ~$15K in incremental profit.

KPIs to track: Reactivation rate, profit per reactivated customer, retention cost vs CLV uplift.
